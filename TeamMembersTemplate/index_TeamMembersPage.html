Dr. Arghya Kusum Das

Arghya Kusum Das (Ph.D.) is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at UAF's College of Engineering and Management (CEM), specializing in large-scale cyber infrastructure development for scientific big data analysis and AI, particularly in bioinformatics and healthcare.

His research combines scalable software development and HPC-based hardware infrastructure, crucial for establishing the AI/ML research foundation at UAF.

Dr. Das has strong collaborations with UAF scientists, including those in Biology, and with the UAF Supercomputing Center.

As PI, he has received multiple grants from federal agencies such as NSF, DOE, NASA, NIH (AIM-AHEAD), and USGS.

He has also earned numerous university awards, including the UAF Center ICE Arctic Fellow award and the UAF State R1 Mentorship award.

Dr. Das established the Data/AI Lab at UAF, fostering interdisciplinary research in big data analysis and AI, and rapidly expanding its research scope through collaborations across the university and the state of Alaska.
